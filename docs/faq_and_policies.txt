GREENFIELD UNIVERSITY
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS & POLICIES

ADMISSIONS FAQ

Q: What is the acceptance rate at Greenfield University?
A: Greenfield University has an acceptance rate of approximately 42%. We are a selective institution that considers the whole student, not just grades and test scores.

Q: Is the SAT/ACT required?
A: Greenfield University is test-optional for the 2024-2025 admissions cycle. Students may submit SAT or ACT scores, but they are not required. If submitted, scores are considered holistically.

Q: Can I defer my admission?
A: Yes, admitted students may defer enrollment for one semester or one full year. Submit a deferral request within 30 days of acceptance. A $500 deferral deposit is required.

Q: What is the student-to-faculty ratio?
A: Greenfield University maintains a 16:1 student-to-faculty ratio, ensuring personalized attention and engagement.

Q: Are campus tours available?
A: Yes! Campus tours are offered Monday through Saturday. Book online at tours.greenfield.edu. Virtual tours are available 24/7.

Q: How do I apply for transfer admission?
A: Transfer students must have completed at least 24 credit hours at an accredited institution with a minimum GPA of 2.8. Submit official transcripts from all colleges attended.

FINANCIAL AID FAQ

Q: When is the FAFSA deadline?
A: We recommend submitting the FAFSA by February 15 for priority consideration. Our FAFSA school code is 002198. Late applications are accepted but funding may be limited.

Q: Are scholarships renewable?
A: Most merit scholarships are renewable for four years, provided students maintain the required GPA (typically 3.0+ for continued eligibility).

Q: Can I work on campus?
A: Yes, work-study positions are available. Students may work up to 20 hours per week during the semester. Common positions include library assistant, lab aide, and office worker.

Q: What if my financial situation changes mid-year?
A: Contact the Financial Aid Office immediately at finaid@greenfield.edu or (555) 234-5680. Special Circumstance reviews are available.

ACADEMIC FAQ

Q: What is the minimum GPA to remain in good academic standing?
A: Students must maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher. Students below 2.0 are placed on academic probation for one semester.

Q: How many credits are required to graduate?
A: Undergraduate students must complete 120 credit hours. Requirements vary by program but generally include:
- 40 credits of general education requirements
- 40-60 credits in the major
- 20-40 elective credits

Q: Can I take courses at other universities?
A: Yes, through our consortium agreements with 5 local universities, students may take up to 12 credits from approved partner institutions. Approval from the registrar is required.

Q: What is the add/drop policy?
A: Students may add or drop courses without penalty during the first two weeks of the semester. After week 2, a "W" (withdrawal) grade is recorded. After week 10, withdrawal requires dean's approval.

Q: Are there honors sections available?
A: Yes, Honors sections are available for many introductory courses. Enrollment is limited to Honors Program students.

CAMPUS LIFE FAQ

Q: Is Greenfield a dry campus?
A: Yes, Greenfield University is a dry campus. Possession or consumption of alcohol is prohibited for all students, regardless of age. Violations result in disciplinary action.

Q: What is the campus safety situation like?
A: Greenfield University has its own campus police department, operating 24/7. The campus is equipped with emergency blue-light phones and a mobile safety app (GreenAlert).

Q: What healthcare services are available?
A: The Student Health Center offers primary care, mental health counseling, and pharmacy services. Hours are Mon-Fri 8 AM - 6 PM, urgent care Sat-Sun 10 AM - 4 PM. All enrolled students have access.

Q: What dining options are there on campus?
A: There are 6 dining locations on campus:
- Main Dining Hall (all-you-can-eat, open daily)
- The Greenfield Cafe (coffee and light meals)
- The Commons (food court style)
- STEM Bistro (near engineering building)
- Arts Cafe (near fine arts building)
- 24/7 Market (convenience store with hot foods)

POLICIES

Academic Integrity Policy:
Greenfield University takes academic integrity seriously. Plagiarism, cheating, or fabrication will result in:
- First offense: Failure on the assignment/exam and mandatory integrity workshop
- Second offense: Failure in the course
- Third offense: Suspension or expulsion

Attendance Policy:
Regular attendance is expected. Missing more than 20% of a class may result in an automatic "F" grade. Excused absences require documentation.

Privacy Policy:
Student records are protected under FERPA. Greenfield University does not share student information with third parties without written consent, except as required by law.

Non-Discrimination Policy:
Greenfield University is committed to equal opportunity and does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability, or veteran status.
